About Me

I'm a Computer Science graduate from California State University, Long Beach, with a genuine curiosity for how systems are designed — not just how to build them, but why they're built the way they are.

After graduating, I spent nearly a year in AI data operations at Alignerr, evaluating large language model outputs at scale. That role gave me an uncommon perspective: I understand not just how to write software, but how the AI increasingly embedded in that software gets trained, where it fails, and why. Most engineers my level haven't had that exposure.

In my own time, I read engineering post-mortems from Cloudflare, Stripe, and Netflix. I tend to over-document everything — my personal projects have READMEs that are probably more thorough than they need to be. In collaborative environments, that instinct tends to be an asset.

Outside of code, I run distance — which I mention because it reflects how I work: comfortable with long unglamorous stretches of effort, no external pressure required.
